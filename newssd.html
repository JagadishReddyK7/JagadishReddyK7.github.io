About This Course

Software System Development is a graduate-level course that equips students with a comprehensive understanding of the principles, tools, and practices involved in designing and building modern software systems. Emphasizing a systems-oriented perspective, the course explores foundational and contemporary topics including Operating Systems, Computer Networks, Software Engineering methodologies, and Database Systems (both relational and NoSQL).

Students will gain practical experience with industry-relevant tools and technologies such as Bash scripting, SQL, NoSQL databases, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, front-end frameworks, RESTful APIs, Python, and Flask. The course focuses on building scalable, maintainable, and secure software systems while fostering an appreciation for system integration, version control, development workflows, and deployment strategies.

Designed for students with a fundamental background in programming, this course bridges theory and practice, preparing them to contribute effectively to complex software development projects in both research and industry settings.

Course Policy [go to top]

  • Teaching and Learning Methodology: This course is designed to help learners get the essence of principles behind good software system development. Learners will be taught concepts by making them work on real-life problems. This will promote deeper engagement, deeper understanding and resembles the true career situation. Overall Course Notes can be found here.
  • Academic Honesty: If A helps B in a Task X, B doesn't get the opportunity to do task X and doesn't learn the required skill. This makes B unfit for jobs requiring those skills. You may think it is okay to do it only once, but when a task is done once it gets wired into the brain as being "okay". If you want to help, help them learn!
  • Zero Tolerance on Plagiarism: This course requires students to submit assignments, participate in activities, and work on projects. Any form of cheating or plagiarism will result in a score of “0” for the task. Egregious cases will result in a grade of “F” for the course.
  • Absentee policy: You are expected to attend and participate in all lectures and lab sessions. If any quiz/activity is held during a session you are absent from, you will be given a ‘0’ for the same. There will be NO make-up classes/quizzes/activities.
  • Miscellaneous Instructions: Given the subject is based on building software systems, ensure you have a laptop with a decent configuration. Please make every effort to participate in discussions and interact with the instructor and TAs.
  • Use of AI Tools: Students are free to use AI tools like ChatGPT, Co-pilot, etc., for course assignments. However, you must mention what tools were used and how they were used in your submission report. Please note that these tools can be misleading. It's important to know the concepts before using the tools.
  • Submission Deadlines: There will be NO extensions for any graded task. It is your responsibility to submit on time. We will not entertain last-minute issues. If you couldn't submit due to health issues or other valid reasons, please work with the academic office for an exemption.
  • Grading Policy: Once a grade is released, students may request a re-evaluation (with appropriate reasons) up to one week after the grade has been posted. After that, grades are final. Late submissions will not be entertained without an exemption from the academic office.
